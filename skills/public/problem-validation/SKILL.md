# Problem Validation — Founder OS

Validates that a real, painful problem exists before building solutions. Integrates the Pressure Profile to make interviews pressure-aware, not just pain-aware.

> **42% of failed startups shut down because they built products with no market need.** This skill prevents that.

---

## Core Philosophy

### The Mom Test
**Never ask**: "Do you think this is a good idea?"
**Always ask**: "Tell me about the last time you dealt with [problem]."

Past behavior doesn't lie. Politeness does.

### Three Validations Required

| Validation | Question | Evidence |
|------------|----------|----------|
| **Problem exists** | Is this real? | Stories of past pain |
| **Problem is painful** | Painful enough to pay/change? | Money/time on alternatives |
| **You can solve it** | Can your approach help? | Early user feedback |

---

## Phase 1: Pressure-Aware Problem Hypothesis

Before interviews, map not just the pain but the **pressure context** (see `gtm-strategy` Pressure Profile). This makes interviews dramatically sharper.

```markdown
## Problem Hypothesis

Target Customer: [Specific person, specific context]

Problem Statement:
[Customer] struggles with [problem] when [situation],
which causes [negative consequence].

Pressure Context:
- Organizational pressure: [What's the company going through?]
- Role pressure: [What's their function dealing with?]
- Personal pressure: [What is this person stressed about?]
- Temporal trigger: [When does this become urgent?]

Current Workarounds:
- Option 1: [Solution] — [Limitation]
- Option 2: [Solution] — [Limitation]
- Do nothing: [Cost of inaction]

Key Assumptions to Test:
1. [Assumption about the problem]
2. [Assumption about pressure context]
3. [Assumption about willingness to pay/change]
```

## Phase 2: Customer Discovery Interviews

### Interview Structure (30–45 min)

1. Context (5 min): Understand their world and current pressure
2. Problem exploration (15–20 min): Deep dive on the pain
3. Current solutions (10 min): What they do today
4. Quantification (5 min): How much it costs them
5. Commitment test (5 min): Would they act on a solution?

### Question Bank

**Context + Pressure Opening:**
- "Walk me through what you're focused on this quarter."
- "What's changed in your work in the last 6 months?"
- "What's keeping you up at night right now?" — unlocks pressure context

**Problem Exploration:**
- "Tell me about the last time you dealt with [problem]."
- "Walk me through it step by step — what happened?"
- "How often does this come up?"
- "What's the worst part about it?"

**Pressure Quantification:**
- "How does this affect your team/numbers/goals?"
- "What would happen if this didn't get solved this quarter?"
- "Is this on your radar right now or is it a background annoyance?"

**Current Solutions:**
- "How do you handle this today?"
- "What have you tried that didn't work?"
- "How much time/money are you spending on alternatives?"

**Willingness to Act:**
- "If I could solve this, would you want to know more?"
- "Who else should I talk to about this?"
- "Would you try an early version?"

### Signal Strength Guide

**Strong signals**
- Already tried to solve it (and failed)
- Paying for alternatives right now
- Describes emotional frustration unprompted
- Can quantify the cost immediately
- Asks when your solution is ready
- Pressure context aligns with your hypothesis

**Weak signals**
- "That would be nice to have"
- Can't remember a specific instance
- Problem solved by 5-minute workaround
- Generic agreement, no specifics

**Red flags**
- "I don't really have that problem"
- Wouldn't pay anything for a solution
- Multiple interviews reveal completely different problems
- The pressure context doesn't match your hypothesis

## Phase 3: Must-Have vs. Nice-to-Have

### Priority Matrix

| | High Frequency | Low Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **High Severity** | Must-Have (Tier 1) | Important (Tier 2) |
| **Low Severity** | Nice-to-Have (Tier 3) | Ignore |

### Must-Have Criteria (ALL must be true)

1. Users can't accomplish core goal without it
2. Multiple users mentioned it unprompted
3. Users are currently paying/struggling to solve it
4. It's a dealbreaker in buying decision

## Phase 4: Willingness to Pay

### Van Westendorp Test (4 Questions)

1. At what price would this be **too expensive** to consider?
2. At what price would this be so **cheap** you'd question quality?
3. At what price would it **start to feel expensive** but you'd still consider?
4. At what price would this be a **bargain**?

The acceptable price range = overlap between questions 1 and 3.

### Commitment Ladder

| Commitment | Test | Signal |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Letter of Intent | "Sign a non-binding LOI?" | Very Strong |
| Deposit | "Refundable deposit?" | Very Strong |
| Pilot | "30-day pilot?" | Strong |
| Demo | "Demo next week?" | Medium |
| Intro | "Who else should I talk to?" | Medium |
| Verbal | "Sounds interesting" | Weak |
